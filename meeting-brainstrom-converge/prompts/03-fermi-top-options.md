# Fermi Estimate Top Options

Using the Fermi estimation method, evaluate the top 2-3 ideas from our brainstorm-converge session. For each idea, estimate the following:

1. **Cost to implement** — Break down into time (person-weeks) and money (tools, licenses, contractors)
2. **Potential ticket deflection** — What percentage of the 1,400 weekly tickets could this eliminate? What is the absolute number per week?
3. **Annual cost savings** — Based on reduced agent time, fewer tickets, or efficiency gains
4. **Time to first impact** — How many weeks until we see the first measurable improvement?

**Anchors from the meeting (use these in your estimates):**
- 1,400 tickets per week
- 22 minutes average handle time per ticket
- 6 support agents at $65K/year each ($390K total labor)
- 30% of tickets are onboarding questions
- Current tools: Zendesk, Notion, Slack

**For each estimate:**
- Decompose into estimable components (show your math)
- Provide a range: optimistic, realistic, pessimistic
- Sanity-check the final number against a known benchmark or alternate calculation path

**Output format:**
Present each idea as a Fermi card:

```
IDEA: [Name]
---------------------------------------------
Cost to implement:    $XX,XXX  (X person-weeks)
Ticket deflection:    XX% (~XXX tickets/week)
Annual savings:       $XXX,XXX
Time to first impact: X weeks
Confidence:           [Low / Medium / High]
Key assumption:       [The one thing that most affects this estimate]
```
