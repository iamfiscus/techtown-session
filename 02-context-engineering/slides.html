5 Types of Context in One Email

A client emails asking to reschedule a meeting. To reply well, you need:

#Context NeededWhy It Matters
1Original meeting detailsWhat was it about?
2Your calendarWhen are you free?
3Their priority levelVIP client or cold lead?

The Email Test

Same Prompt. Different Context.

Zero Context Generic Garbage

"Thank you for reaching out. We'd be happy to help. Please let us know your availability."
Same reply for every customer. No personalization. No value.

Rich Context Nails It

"Hi Sarah — since you've been on our Pro plan for 2 years, I've applied the loyalty discount and rescheduled your onboarding to Thursday 2pm."
Personalized. Proactive. Shows it knows the customer.

"You wouldn't ask a new hire to reply to that email on their first day. Stop doing that to AI."

5 / 30 TechTown Start Studio | Session 2
Context Framework

4 Operations of Context Engineering

Write
Save context outside the window
Take notes so you don't forget
Save to Obsidian, document decisions, create specs
Select
Pull the RIGHT context in
Grab the relevant folder, not the whole cabinet
Search notes, retrieve docs, RAG
Compress
Keep only what matters
Summarize a 2-hour meeting into 5 bullets
Summarize threads, trim history
Isolate
Split context for different tasks
Don't bring your grocery list to a board meeting
Separate chats per project, fresh context

"Context engineering is doing these 4 things deliberately instead of accidentally."

Context Hydration & The Attention Budget

Context hydration = deliberately filling the context window with the right information before AI acts.

Imagine reading a 500-page manual to fix a leaky faucet vs. reading the 2-page section on faucets.

AI Accuracy
Amount of Context Added

7 / 30 TechTown Start Studio | Session 2
Attention Budget

Maximum vs. Optimal Context

Too Much Entire Codebase

*Pastes 50,000 lines of code* "Fix the bug"
AI gets lost. Gives generic advice. Misses the actual problem. Wastes your attention budget on irrelevant files.

Just Right Relevant File + Error

*Pastes the 200-line file + error message* "Fix the bug on line 47"
AI focuses. Identifies the bug immediately. Suggests the exact fix. High-signal, low-noise.

"The goal isn't maximum context. It's optimal context."

Context Rot

Context Rot

You're on a 2-hour phone call. By minute 90, you've forgotten what was said in minute 10. AI has the same problem.

What context rot looks like:

  • Long conversations get worse over time
  • Important instructions from early in the chat get "buried"
  • AI starts contradicting its own earlier answers
  • Stale information misleads decisions

Session 1 Connection

"Every bad prompt, every retry stays in context and pollutes future responses." Now you understand the mechanics.

Obsidian

Obsidian + Vector Brain — "The Second Brain"

Solves: Write — persistent context that survives across sessions

Kitchen Table

Your notes are scattered Post-its. A vector brain is a filing cabinet that also understands what the notes MEAN and how they connect.

Founder Desk

  • Take your Obsidian vault (all markdown notes)
  • Create vector embeddings (GPS coordinates for ideas)
  • Store in a GitHub repo or gist
  • AI searches by MEANING, not just keywords
  • Ask "What decisions have I made about pricing?" — answers from 6 months ago

SuperDesign

SuperDesign — "The Design Shortcut"

Solves: Compress + Isolate — structuring intent before building

Kitchen Table

Instead of describing what you want a room to look like in 500 words, you show a photo and say "like this, but blue."

Founder Desk

  • Describe a dashboard in plain language
  • Get 10+ design variants
  • Pick one → get production-ready code
  • Open source, runs in VS Code/Cursor, works offline

Context Engineering Connection

Design compresses your intent into a visual artifact. That visual becomes high-signal context for the build phase. You isolate "what should it look like" from "how do I build it."

Decision Framework

Which Tool Solves My Problem?

What do I need?
├── Research across many docs? → NotebookLM
├── Remember things across sessions? → Obsidian + Vector Brain
├── Connect AI to live tools & data? → MCP
├── Automate context assembly? → N8N
└── Plan visually before building? → SuperDesign
What operation am I performing?
├── Need to save context? → Write (Obsidian, docs, GitHub)
├── Need to retrieve context? → Select (NotebookLM, search, RAG)
├── Need to trim context? → Compress (summarize, fresh chats)
└── Need to separate tasks? → Isolate (new chats, project separation)
