# Grocery List Analogy

> "Don't bring your grocery list to a board meeting."

## The Analogy

Imagine walking into a board meeting about company strategy with your grocery list in hand. Every time someone asks about Q3 projections, your brain glances at "eggs, milk, bread."

It's not that you CAN'T focus on strategy. It's that irrelevant context is competing for your attention.

## What It Explains

This makes the [[isolate-operation]] instantly intuitive. When you mix unrelated contexts:
- AI's [[attention-budget]] is split across irrelevant topics
- Project A's context interferes with Project B's work
- Instructions for one task bleed into another
- Quality drops for ALL tasks, not just the contaminated one

## The Solution

Separate contexts for separate tasks:
- Strategy discussion → Strategy chat
- Product development → Product chat
- Customer support → Support chat
- Grocery shopping → Grocery list (different app entirely!)

## Context Engineering Connection

This analogy motivates the [[isolate-operation]]:
- Each project/task gets its own conversation
- No cross-contamination between topics
- [[attention-budget]] is fully available for the current task
- [[context-rot]] is isolated to one conversation, not spread everywhere

## Common Objection

> "But I don't want to lose context by starting a new chat!"

The fix: [[write-operation]] + [[select-operation]]. Save important context externally first, then start fresh and re-inject only what's relevant. You keep the signal, lose the noise.
