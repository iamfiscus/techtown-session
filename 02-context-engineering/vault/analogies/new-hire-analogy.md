# New Hire Analogy

> "You wouldn't ask a new hire to reply to that email on their first day. Stop doing that to AI."

## The Analogy

Imagine hiring someone new. On their very first day, before any onboarding, you hand them a client email and say "reply to this."

They'd have no idea about:
- Your company's voice and policies ([[static-context]])
- The client's history with you ([[episodic-context]])
- Current priorities and workload ([[dynamic-context]])
- Access to your CRM or calendar ([[tool-context]])

The reply would be **generic garbage**. Not because the new hire is bad — because they have no context.

## The Point

This is what you do to AI every time you start a conversation without engineering the context first.

AI without context = new hire on day one.
AI with engineered context = tenured employee who knows everything.

## What It Teaches

1. **Context is the difference** — Not ability, not intelligence. Context.
2. **You'd never skip onboarding for a human** — Don't skip it for AI
3. **[[context-hydration]] = onboarding** — Brief AI like you'd brief an employee
4. **The "prompt" isn't the problem** — The lack of context is

## Context Engineering Connection

This analogy is the emotional hook for why [[context-engineering]] matters. It reframes AI context from a technical problem to a **management problem** that founders already understand.

Good managers provide:
- Brand docs and procedures → [[static-context]]
- Access to live tools → [[dynamic-context]] via [[mcp]]
- Historical context → [[episodic-context]] via [[obsidian-vector-brain]]
- Tool training → [[tool-context]]
