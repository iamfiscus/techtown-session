# Filing Cabinet Analogy

> "Your notes are scattered Post-its. A vector brain is a filing cabinet that also understands what the notes MEAN and how they connect."

## The Analogy

### Post-its on the Fridge
- Notes everywhere, no organization
- Can only find things by remembering WHERE you put them
- No connections between related notes
- If the note falls off, it's gone

### Regular Filing Cabinet
- Organized by folders
- Can find things by LOCATION (which folder?)
- Better than Post-its, but still rigid
- Have to know the right folder name

### Vector Brain Filing Cabinet
- Organized by MEANING
- Can find things by WHAT THEY'RE ABOUT (not just where they are)
- "What decisions have I made about pricing?" finds notes you didn't even know were related
- Connections between ideas are automatic

## What It Explains

This analogy makes [[vector-embeddings]] and [[obsidian-vector-brain]] intuitive:
1. **Post-its** = scattered notes with no system (most founders' situation)
2. **Regular cabinet** = basic folder organization (Google Docs, Notion)
3. **Vector brain** = semantic understanding of your knowledge (Obsidian + embeddings)

## The Upgrade Path

Most founders are at the "Post-it" or "regular cabinet" stage. The vector brain is the next level — your knowledge becomes **searchable by meaning**, not just location or keywords.

## Context Engineering Connection

This analogy explains the [[write-operation]] + [[select-operation]] loop:
- **Write** = Put notes IN the filing cabinet
- **Select** = Find the RIGHT notes when you need them
- Vector brain makes Select **dramatically better** because it searches by meaning

Also relates to:
- [[second-brain]] — The vector brain IS your second brain
- [[knowledge-graphs]] — The connections between notes form a graph
- [[rag]] — The retrieval mechanism that makes this work
